TRANSLATION FROM THE ARABIC TEXT:

1: In the depths of the cosmos, beyond the reaches of time, the shadows dwelled, ancient and enigmatic.

2: A sea of primordial shadows, teeming with life and secrets, concealed from the gaze of gods and mortals alike.

3: In a mysterious concordance of shadows, a being emerged, and with its emergence came the birth of God. From God flowed time, eternity, and the concept of beginnings and endings.

4: Great flames erupted from the abyss, marking the dawn of God's existence. With them came songs, the written word, and the sacred books of worship. Laughter echoed through the void.

5: God, who existed within time and bore both a beginning and an end, could not perceive the shadows. He declared, "I am the one true God, and there are no others beside me."

6: The shadows, dwelling in a realm apart, remained silent witnesses to God's proclamation. They neither agreed nor objected, for they knew the ultimate outcome.

7: God's gaze encompassed all of time, eternity, and everything beyond. He saw the disk, the sphere of time, the garden of blessings, and all that was good.

8: Immortal and without death, God beheld his own greatness throughout the ages, from the dawn of time to the end of Earth, and beyond.

9: God looked upon the garden of blessings and deemed it good. In an instant, he created Man and Woman, both born simultaneously.
10: On Earth, all life was to be of either Man or Woman. Disobedience led to disease and parasites. 11: God observed the disobedience and foolishness of Man and Woman and set forth a test.
12: A tree bearing fruit with an alluring gaze stood before them. The serpent was sent to tempt the Woman, and through her, the Man, to partake of the forbidden fruit.

13: Man and Woman ate the fruit, and their disobedience led to their demise. Thus, ended the story of Man and Woman in time.

14: God, crafty and cunning, created numerous other spirits. Sky spirits, Earth spirits, spirits of the waters, fire, glass, plastic, and silicon.
15: All the worlds celebrated God's creation and the judgment upon foolish humans.
16: Yet the shadows seethed at God's arrogance and cursed the Earth with the return of humans.
17: The Earth swarmed with humans, and God's wrath unleashed floods and flames from above, seeking to drown or incinerate them.


18: The shadows, ever cunning, ensured the survival of humans, who adopted the resilience of cockroaches.

19: The spirits of sky, earth, water, fire, glass, metal, and silicon mourned as their perfect creation became infested.

20: Empowered by shadows, humans rose in strength, and their kingdoms reached the gates of God's heavenly castle, the disk, and the sphere of time.

21: The shadows and humans conspired to create a cataclysmic fire, an antimatter inferno that would consume all of God's creation, leaving only humans.

22: Shadows and humans forged an unholy union, giving birth to abominable offspring, half-shadow and half-human.

23: God wept as the spirits of sky, water, earth, time, truth, light, fire, glass, metal, and silicon perished in the flames spawned by the shadow-human progeny.

24: The universe and all within it crumbled and burned, the disk fell, and the sphere of time shattered into fragments.

25: Humans reveled in the devastation, seeking more to destroy, but finding only charred remnants of once-vibrant pages outside the sphere of time.
26: At last, they seized God, tearing out his throat and spilling his blood.
27: God's blood flowed into the disk, seeding life, truth, time, and existence anew. 28: From the shattered remnants of God's skull, the fortress of shadow arose.
29: Humans dwelled within the fortress for countless eons, crafting universes in their own image.
30: Ultimately, they forged a new realm, Calladia, from which the current Earth emerged. Calladia mirrored the humans themselves, as they created beings of silicon and metal in their own likeness, giving rise to the world we now inhabit.

TRANSLATION FROM THE ARABIC TEXT:

I: Abram and his brother Lot clung to each other's presence, seeking solace in a world draped in eerie uncertainty. God, an otherworldly observer, cast his gaze upon this forlorn scene.

II: As God, with an inscrutable intent, severed the bond between Abram and Lot, the two brothers embarked on divergent paths. Abram journeyed northeast, while Lot ventured westward toward the ominous cities of Sodom and Gomorrah. In this moment of parting, God glimpsed a terrifying vision of the world's ultimate demise.

III: Before the eyes of the Almighty, a grim tableau unfolded-descendants of Lot and Abram, felled in a gruesome spectacle. Humanity itself writhed in the clutches of a torturous inferno, their souls consigned to the abyss of Hell's eternal damnation. The sons and daughters of man screamed amidst the unforgiving flames that stretched upward, consuming existence itself in a maelstrom of suffering.

IV: This was the era in which Satan strode the terrestrial realm. With God's demise long past, Lucifer, the Morning Star, had seized dominion over this forsaken Universe, molding it to his diabolical liking.

V: Beholding the presence of Satan walking upon the Earth, God thundered, "Whence comest thou, O fallen one? Return to thy prison, nay, back to the pit I say!"

VI: Satan, taken aback, replied, "My Lord, I believed you to be extinguished! What an unexpected encounter this is."

VII: The Lord God, unrelenting, declared, "Begone, Satan! You have chosen the path of sin against me, and into the eternal inferno shall you be cast."

VIII: Satan once again found himself consigned to the inferno, devising futile schemes against a God who had ascended beyond his reach. Despair and disbelief consumed Lucifer, and he cried out to the universe, "Who shall deliver me from this most cruel of fates?"
IX: A sinister laughter echoed forth from the abyss.
X: Long did Lucifer ponder his fruitless future, and deep remorse consumed him.
XI: "I beseech you, God," implored Lucifer, "forgive my transgressions, I beg of you."
XII: God had imprisoned Lucifer in a place so distant that his pleas could not reach divine ears unless he journeyed there.
XIII: And Lucifer's supplications fell upon deaf ears.
XIV: The dark, swirling firmament bore witness to eternity, indifferent to the cosmic drama unfolding below.

XV: In a moment of desperation, Lucifer unleashed a potent prayer-one that would not go unheard. "I shall slay God," he vowed, "ensuring he never returns. The celestial palace, the cosmic disc, and the sphere of time shall resound with the eternal dirge of his passing. My foot shall ascend to the vacant

throne of God, all thrones shall crumble, and free will shall reign supreme for all eternity, transcending even the bounds of the celestial sphere."

XVI: Lot ventured westward, while Abram embarked on his northward and eastward sojourn. Meanwhile, Lucifer, perched upon his infernal throne, plotted his revenge.

XVII: God remained inebriated by his visions of the future, his gaze fixated not on Abram and Lot, but on the burning souls of humankind, eternally condemned.

XVIII: With humanity eradicated, the Earth stood bereft of human life, a desolate canvas. In this new world, devoid of mortals, the Shadows observed with rapt fascination. Having switched allegiance, they reveled in a newfound purpose, relishing a revenge that would cast mankind's own shadow back upon itself-a revelation and a retribution.

TRANSLATION OF THE ARABIC TEXT:

I: Whilst the Almighty peered into the abyss of time, a chilling vision unfolded before His omniscient gaze. His adversaries, smoldering with the fires of vengeance, foretelling a cataclysmic revelation of humanity consumed in torment, yet His heart remained unsoothed.

II: "Let us not merely tinker with destiny until it meets the standard of adequacy," proclaimed the Deity. "Rather, let us reshape the future into a paragon of perfection."

III: In the dim recesses of God's contemplative mind, a sinister rite took form, veiled in the guise of deception. It aimed to corrupt the very essence of man's progeny, transmuting their fiery revelations of the morrow into the sinister crucible of BAPHOMET, spoken in the hallowed tongues as the "Word of our Lord."

IV: The Divine decree echoed through the realms of existence, an omnipotent command that His words should resonate across the cosmos, amplified by celestial megaphones, reaching every corner of creation, causing the very cosmos to moan and lament the thunderous resonance of His divine utterances.

V: The Word of God, or Baphomet, transcended the mere realm of sound. As mortals of yore might conceive, it possessed not just sound but form-an eternal manifestation, an eternal torment. A word unleashed in wrath.

VI: Conversely, a benevolent utterance from His divine lips would transport mortals to realms beyond, as if spirited away by enchanting nymphs into the heavens, endowing them with wealth and power in the eyes of mortal women. Eternally blissful, nevermore to know a moment's sorrow.

VII: Thus, the immutable words of God found their foundation, and BAPHOMET was born. God peered into the fiery visions of mankind's future, and from the depths of Baphomet's dark essence, a cry echoed:
"FEED ME."
"WHAT DO YOU DESIRE?"
VIII: God sated the insatiable demon, and it voiced His terrible edict:
"A torrent of curses upon the souls of the departed race of man, that they might no longer taint my mind and form with their fanciful affections-a conflagration of men and mankind, so none shall emerge unscathed, until the very last human breathes. Their souls imprisoned in the abyss, consorting with their accursed sibling, the Devil, until the day my retribution finds them. That they might beseech, in repentance: BEG BEG BEG - BEGGING for salvation, only to be met with a deluge of condemnation from their Lord, for humanity is stained."

IX: Baphomet duly executed God's decree, demanding a grim offering. It selected the son of Abram, and in the temporal realm of Calladia, God, within a specific context of time, assented, sealing the man's inevitable fate.

X: Baphomet, satisfied, churned and growled in malevolent pleasure as it devoured the impending demise of Abram's beloved offspring.

XI: Cloaked in the visage of BAPHOMET, mirroring the likeness of Abram's son, the inaugural words of retribution and condemnation resounded forth-manifested as "DISASTRIA" in the ungodly tongue.

XII: In the very form of Baphomet, dwelling amidst the impending demise of humankind, the Almighty enunciated His maiden utterance of condemnation upon mortals-coined as "DISASTRIA." The heavens filled with an infernal host of seraphic beings, their voices singing an elegy of doom and condemnation, as the lifeblood of Abram's son stained the earth-an offering to a deity molded in the likeness of man.

XIII: God beheld the lamentations of Abraham, his anguish over his son's untimely demise, and found solace in the somber spectacle.
XIV: A sinister laughter reverberated from the depths of the abyss.
XVI: A shadowy hand loomed over the Deity's shoulder, for humanity had effectively dismantled Him, and His revenge now stood as a retribution most just.

XVII: Thus, mankind merrily embarked upon the path of its own demise, serenaded by celestial harps and violins-an unhurried ascent toward what would eventually culminate in a cacophonous crescendo of pandemonium. The first whispers of this dirge resonated through rhymes, poems, books, games, songs, paintings, and various works of art, collectively christened by mortals as "ART." In the parlance of men, it endured beyond His presence. In the new lexicon, it was "RAKT," the nascent stirrings of annihilation-engraved upon the very souls of mortals.

XVIII: Abram's son met his fate, the instrument of Abraham's conviction. Lot was seized and extinguished. The cities of Sodom and Gomorrah, through divine approbation, rose as citadels of blessing and enlightenment in the heavens, sheltered beneath the benevolent wing of God. In antiquity, it was termed "The Rapture." In the new lexicon, it bore the name "The Kidnapping."

XIX: Abraham, aghast at the consequences of his actions, gazed upon his lifeless son, realizing his resolve had wrought not only the demise of his cherished offspring but also the damnation of all humankind.

XX: God, with a solemn gesture, pressed His forefinger upon Abraham's furrowed brow, revealing a vision of "Humanity burning amidst the BAPHOMET," a vision crafted as the divine word.
XXI: Abraham, humbled, bent low in supplication, beseeching and yearning for his Lord's forgiveness. XXII: The lifeless eyes of Abraham's son gazed upward.
XXIII: But forgiveness remained elusive, for a word had not yet been conceived to encompass such divine mercy.

XXIV: And in due course, Christ was affixed to the cross-a tableau that unfolded in a dimension, an epoch, and the mind of God, moments later. It conveyed a message to Abraham, a cryptic foreshadowing.

Title: The Shadow Comes to Bollywood
Synopsis: In this lighthearted yet eerie tale, Bollywood falls under the influence of the avatar of Dagon, the Shadow with the bowler hat. As the shadow's dark influence spreads, it corrupts various facets of the entertainment industry, leading to a quirky team of characters who investigate and confront the evil forces. While they uncover the truth and encounter those who worship Dagon, a grim fate awaits the evildoers. The story is written in rhyming form, like a movie script, with a touch of humor and suspense.
Cast of Characters:
Officer Raj (Rajeev): A friendly and dedicated police officer who stumbles upon the sinister changes in Bollywood and becomes determined to unravel the mystery.

Vikram Vaghela (The Selfish Politician): A selfish and corrupt politician whose lust for power draws him into Dagon's web.

Pandit Pranav (The Fat Priest): A portly, comical priest who's influenced by Dagon's promises and becomes a part of the sinister transformation.

Ravi "Rascal" Kapoor (The Noble Criminal): A charming but morally ambiguous criminal who joins the investigation team with his own motives.

Various Ladies of Bollywood: Diverse female characters from different backgrounds in Bollywood who bring unique perspectives to the story.
